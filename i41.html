Manufacturer's History

BMW, which stands for Bayerische Motoren Werke, originated in 1916 as Bayerische Flugzeug-Werke, a builder of aircraft engines. It began producing motorcycles in the 1920s and entered the automobile business in 1928. The company's R32 motorcycle set a world speed record that stood until 19371. BMW was created from the Munich firm Rapp-Motorenwerke and was incorporated into Knorr-Bremse AG in 1920 before being refounded as BMW AG in 19222.
